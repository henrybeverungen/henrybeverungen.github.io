### What Does it Mean?

Draft:

The ship of theseus is a common philisophical problem from ancient
greece that asks if something is the same after every individual
part in it has been replaced over time. The ancient Greek example
uses a ship, but most of us have probably experienced this
philisophical conundrum with an item in our lives: the desktop
computer. So, I would like to ask, what's your take on it? Is your
desktop the same computer when you switch out the fans? Replace the
GPU with a new one? The power supply? What about the heart of the
system? The CPU and motherboard? What about a motherboard that can
support multiple generations of CPUs? AM4 boards have 5 Generations
of CPUs on them now! Now I have just built my first computer but
parts have already been swapped out. Swapping to an AIO as opposed
to air cooling. Swapping back. Using the IGPU to having a dedicated
one. So the question I have to ask myself is: when does it stop
being my first computer and start being my second.
